S. M. Iqbal Mahamud
Microbiologist | Bioinformatician
Dept. of Biochemistry & Microbiology
North South University
SIIT, Thammasat University
"
I grew up fascinated by the invisible world — organisms so small you need a microscope to see them, yet powerful enough to shape the fate of entire populations. That curiosity drove me to microbiology, and the more I learned, the more I realized that bacteriophages could be one of the most powerful tools we have against the antimicrobial resistance crisis threatening global health.

I am a microbiologist and computational biologist with a research focus on bacteriophage biology, microbial genomics, and phage–host interactions. My work spans the full pipeline of phage research, including isolation, host-range determination, comparative genomic analysis, and therapeutic evaluation. I am particularly interested in targeting multidrug-resistant pathogens such as Pseudomonas aeruginosa, Citrobacter freundii, and Morganella morganii, contributing to efforts aimed at combating antimicrobial resistance.

I completed my Bachelor's degree in Microbiology from North South University, graduating with Distinction and Cum Laude honors. I have also pursued graduate studies in Biochemical Engineering at Sirindhorn International Institute of Technology (SIIT), Thammasat University. I am currently pursuing my Master's research in Microbiology at Texas Tech University, further advancing my focus on phage therapy, genomics, and microbial biotechnology.

My research has been published in peer-reviewed journals including Current Microbiology and the Journal of Advanced Biotechnology and Experimental Therapeutics, and I have presented my work at national and international conferences in Bangladesh. I am actively interested in collaborative research opportunities in phage therapy, microbial genomics, and translational biotechnology.
